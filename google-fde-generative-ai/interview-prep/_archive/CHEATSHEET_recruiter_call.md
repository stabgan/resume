**Expected (first response):** *"I'd want to understand the level this role is being scoped at before putting a number on it. Could you share the band Google is working with for this requisition?"*

**If he pushes:** *"I'm anchoring on market rather than my current. For L5 IC at Google India, total comp in the ₹95L to ₹1.2Cr range feels fair; L4 would be ₹65–80L total. I'm flexible on the split between base, stock, and bonus."*

**If he wants one number:** *"Around ₹1 crore total, with level determining the split."*

Then stop talking.

## Your 3 questions (pick 2)

1. "Could you walk me through the interview loop — how many rounds, and how's it split between technical and customer-scenario?"
2. "What does the FDE team look like right now — size, reporting structure, and the split between embedded-at-customer work vs. product engineering?"
3. "Is this hire for a specific vertical or customer pattern — enterprise SaaS, public sector, financial services?"

## Red flags — don't say

- Don't badmouth Gracenote, TCS, or J&J. Frame forward, not backward.
- Don't explain ZeRO parallelism or DSPy internals — Harish is a recruiter, not an engineer.
- Don't over-apologize for the ₹33L comp. State it, move on.
- Don't volunteer that you're exploring other roles.
