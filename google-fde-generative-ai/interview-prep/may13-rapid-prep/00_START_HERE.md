# Start Here - May 2 To May 13

You have 11 calendar days. The interview is treated as **May 13**. The target is not to learn everything. The target is to become very hard to reject in two rounds:

1. **RRK, 60 min:** senior production GenAI + customer architecture + troubleshooting.
2. **Coding, 60 min:** clean 30-50 line Python/OOP under no-run conditions.

## The Rule For This Prep

Every day has only three mandatory blocks:

- **Coding:** one no-run Python problem.
- **RRK:** one deep architecture or troubleshooting drill.
- **Recall:** one verbal rehearsal from the cheat sheet or STAR bank.

If work is heavy, do the mandatory blocks and stop. Do not compensate by staying up late.

## Daily Time Budget

Minimum version, 90 minutes:

- 30 min coding.
- 45 min RRK case.
- 15 min recall.

Strong version, 2.5 hours:

- 45 min coding.
- 75 min RRK.
- 30 min recall and debrief.

Do not exceed 3 hours on a workday unless you are unusually fresh.

## What Good Looks Like By May 13

You should be able to:

- Explain FDE in one sentence.
- Design an enterprise RAG or multi-agent system in 20 minutes.
- Explain evals, observability, security, latency, and cost without prompting.
- Solve a medium Python problem in 30-40 minutes without running it.
- Tell 5 stories in 90 seconds each.
- Ask sharp clarifying questions before designing or coding.

## If You Panic

Use this sentence:

> Let me slow down, restate the requirement, and walk through the constraints before I choose the design.

That sentence works in RRK and coding. It turns panic into structure.
